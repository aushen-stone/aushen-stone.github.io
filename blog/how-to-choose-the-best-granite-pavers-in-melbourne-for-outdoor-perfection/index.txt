The space flows, the stone underfoot is cool and solid, and everything looks effortlessly pulled together? Spoiler: It’s not lucky, it’s good planning, and the right choice of materials. If you’re in Melbourne and dreaming of a backyard that makes you want to spend time outside, whether it’s a slick courtyard, poolside hangout or an alfresco dining spot, <strong><a href="https://aushenstone.com.au/granite-pavers-melbourne/">granite pavers in Melbourne</a></strong> might just be your best move. But here’s the catch: not all granite is created equal, and picking the wrong one could turn your outdoor dream into a bit of a headache. Before you rush into a decision based on what looks good online or what’s on sale, take a bet. This guide will walk you through the real stuff, the finishes, the colours, the common mistakes, and what works in Melbourne’s wild weather. Let’s make sure you get it right the first time.</p><h2 id="why-granite"><strong>Why Granite? </strong></h2><p>It’s tough. It holds up to Melbourne’s temper tantrums, 40-degree scorchers, sideways rain, even the odd hailstorm. It doesn’t crack or look weathered after a year. Plus, it has that natural, solid feel that concrete just can’t fake.</p><h2 id="but-not-all-granite-is-the-same"><strong>But Not All Granite Is the Same </strong></h2><p>There are different finishes, and they matter more than you think. Flamed granite has a grip, great for wet areas. The hone finish is smoother and sleeker. And colour? That’s a big deal. Many picked a beautiful dark granite around their pool, looked stunning but felt like walking on a stovetop mid-summer.</p><h2 id="dont-trust-photos-go-check-it"><strong>Don’t Trust Photos- Go Check It </strong></h2><p>Seriously. Lighting changes everything. What looks grey online might look green in your backyard. Grab a sample, chuck it outside, walk it barefoot, even spill a bit of wine on it. That’s how you know if it’s right.</p><h2 id="installation-can-make-or-break-it"><strong>Installation Can Make or Break It </strong></h2><p>There is a possibility that the high-quality granite will shift or stain if the base isn’t prepped right. Avoid the cheapest quotation; instead ask what is included in detail. It’s a big red flag if there’s no mention of drainage or sealing.</p><h2 id="speak-to-someone-who-knows-stone"><strong>Speak To Someone Who Knows Stone! </strong></h2><p>If you’re not sure where to start, it’s worth talking to people who live and breathe this stuff. <strong><a href="https://aushenstone.com.au/">Aushen Stone &amp; Tile</a></strong> in Bayside is a good shout. They’ve been doing this for 20 years and aren’t just selling to sell; they’ll walk you through finishes, sealing, even which stone holds better with kids and dogs running around. They stock high-quality natural stone from around the world, and the team genuinely takes the time to match the right product to your space.
