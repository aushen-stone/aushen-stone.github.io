They don’t just give an attractive appeal to your property, but also provide a stable and decorative surface for a wide range of outdoor areas like patios, driveways or pathways.</p><h4>But Many of You might Wonder - Are Pavers Affordable?</h4><p>Here, we have tried to provide an answer to this question by giving an estimate of how much your outdoor paving may cost:</p><h4>The Cost of Paving Stones</h4><p>Pavers vary significantly in price. There’s a vast variety of different pavers like terracotta, concrete, bricks and natural stones, and each of these come with a different price tag.</p><p><strong>A Quick Tip:</strong> Majority of natural stone products are now significantly cheaper than <a href="https://aushenstone.com.au/concrete-paver/">concrete pavers</a> due to massive increases in manufacturing &amp; labour costs.</p><p>Here, we have outlined some of the <a href="https://aushenstone.com.au/application/stone-paving/">paving</a> options, so to help you get a better idea about the price variations:</p><ul><li>$40 to $70 per square meter for Bluestone paving</li><li>$40 to $80 for granite paving</li><li>$50 to $90 for Travertine paving</li><li>$30 to $70 per square meter for sandstone paving</li><li>$50 to $80 per square metre for limestone paving</li><li>$60 to $90 per square metre for marble paving</li><li>$60 to $100 per square for concrete paving</li></ul><p>You require a strong and stabile base for longevity of stone pavers. Concrete is the ideal base for high volume areas and also the preferred option, crushed rock &amp; sand base is a secondary option for those low volume areas which can be a great way to cut the cost down.</p><p><strong>A Quick Tip:</strong> its best to get your local expert out for the best advice on which option will work for you</p><p>Many other factors determine the overall cost of your outdoor paving. Some of these factors are:</p><ul><li>Labour cost can greatly differ depending size, thickness and material you choose.</li><li>Access and terrain can also impact the overall cost. Paving on a flat surface will cost you less per square metre as compared to a sloping surface.</li><li>Also, the style of paving determines the cost. For example, a driveway with large square pavers would be less expensive compared to the ashlar, French &amp; crazy patterned ones which are made up of small pavers and require more cuts</li></ul><h4>Things to Keep in Mind When Getting Quotes</h4><p>Before you start planning the budget and getting quotes for your outdoor paving project, take some time to work out on the following things:</p><ul><li>The type of pavers would you like to have</li><li>Do you want patterned or simple paving?</li><li>Do you like border pavers?</li><li>Whether you want to pave a flat ground or a sloping surface?</li><li>Figure out the suitable pavers according to your requirement. E.g., if you are paving around a pool, you need a slip-resistant surface.</li></ul><p>Sorting out the points mentioned above will help you compare the quotes and make a well-informed decision.</p><p>We hope this blog will help you plan a budget for an outdoor paving job.</p><p>Do you still have any queries? Feel Free to Talk to the Experts at <a href="https://g.page/aushenstone">Aushen Stone</a>.</p><p><strong>Disclaimer:</strong> Cost mentioned in this blog are indicative.
